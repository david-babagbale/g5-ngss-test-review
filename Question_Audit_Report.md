### üî¥ NEW Q18 ‚Äî Solid vs. Gas Particles (SEVERE)
**PE:** 5-PS1-1 | **DOK:** 1

**Correct answer (F):** "The particles in a solid are locked in place and cannot move, while the particles in a gas move freely in all directions." ‚Äî **22 words**
- Distractor G: "Solids have particles but gases do not contain particles." ‚Äî 9 words
- Distractor H: "Gas particles are lighter than solid particles." ‚Äî 7 words

**Violations:**
1. ‚ö†Ô∏è **Rule 16 ‚Äî Length balance:** SEVERE violation. The correct answer is triple the distractor average. Any test-wise student would select the longest answer.
2. ‚ö†Ô∏è **Rule 20 ‚Äî Homogeneity:** The correct answer is a complex compound sentence; distractors are simple single-clause statements.
3. ‚ö†Ô∏è **Rule 22 ‚Äî Absolute terms:** Distractors G and J use absolute language ("do not contain," "always"), making them easily eliminable.

**Recommended fix:** Lengthen distractors to match the correct answer's two-part structure:

- G) The particles in a solid move freely in all directions, while gas particles are locked in place.
- H) The particles in a solid and a gas both move freely, but gas particles are lighter than solid particles.
- J) The particles in a solid and a gas are both locked in place, but gas particles take up more space.

### üî¥ NEW Q2 ‚Äî Conservation of Mass / Effervescent Tablets (SEVERE)
**PE:** 5-PS1-2 | **DOK:** 2

**Correct answer (G):** "Exactly 65 grams, because matter is not created or destroyed when substances interact" ‚Äî **14 words**
- Distractor F: "Less than 65 grams, because some matter was destroyed when the tablets dissolved" ‚Äî 13 words
- Distractor H: "More than 65 grams, because the gas adds extra mass to the bag" ‚Äî 11 words

**Length ratio:** 1.2x ‚Äî actually acceptable.

**Violations:**
1. ‚ö†Ô∏è **Rule 6 ‚Äî Assumed knowledge:** CRITICAL. The correct answer requires knowing that 50 mL of water = 50 grams (bag 15g + water 50g + tablets = 65g). This density relationship is NOT Grade 5 common knowledge and is NOT stated in the stimulus.
2. ‚ö†Ô∏è **Rule 6 ‚Äî Tablet mass ignored:** The problem mentions "two effervescent tablets" but the correct answer of "exactly 65 grams" doesn't account for the mass of the tablets themselves. The mass should be 15g (bag) + 50g (water) + tablet mass = MORE than 65g.

**Recommended fix:** Restructure to avoid requiring the density conversion and fix the scientific error:

*"A student places two effervescent tablets and water inside a sealed plastic bag and records the total mass as 80 grams. The tablets dissolve and gas bubbles form inside the sealed bag. What will the balance most likely show after the tablets have completely dissolved?"*
- F) Less than 80 grams, because some matter was destroyed
- G) Exactly 80 grams, because matter is not created or destroyed during the change
- H) More than 80 grams, because the gas adds extra mass
- J) Exactly 0 grams, because the tablets dissolved completely

## SYSTEMATIC ISSUES ACROSS ALL 19 QUESTIONS

### Issue 1: Correct Answer is Almost Always the Longest (15/19 = 79%)
The single biggest quality issue. Item writers naturally add qualifiers and precision to the correct answer while writing quick, dismissive distractors. **Fix: After writing, count words in each option. Adjust until within 20%.**

### Issue 2: Distractors Lack Explanatory "Because" Clauses (12/19)
Many correct answers include reasoning ("because X, so Y") while distractors are bare assertions. **Fix: Give every option a "because" clause, or remove the "because" from the correct answer.**

### Issue 3: Absolute Terms Cluster in Distractors (6/19)
Words like "always," "never," "completely," "permanently," "do not contain" appear predominantly in wrong answers. **Fix: Either avoid absolutes entirely or use them equally across all options.**

### Issue 4: One Implausible Distractor per Item (8/19)
About 42% of questions have at least one distractor that no student would ever choose (e.g., "Star B is broken," "air-conditioned playground," "light causes food coloring to move"). **Fix: Replace implausible distractors with documented misconceptions from science education research.**

### Issue 5: Scientific Accuracy (1/19)
N2 has a factual error (ignoring tablet mass in the "exactly 65 grams" answer). **Fix: Always have subject-matter experts verify the scientific accuracy of both the correct answer and all distractors.**
